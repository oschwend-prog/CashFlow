About the waitlist

Check your spam/junk folder first — our welcome email sometimes lands there. Mark it "not spam" to future-proof your delivery.

If it's not there after 30 minutes, email couriers@cashflow.app with the email address you used and we'll look it up manually.

Your position reflects the order in which you joined the waitlist. When London onboarding opens, we'll invite couriers to complete verification starting from position #1.

Positions are by signup timestamp, but referrers who bring in 3 or more sign-ups move ahead of non-referrers — so referring friends is the fastest way to get invited early.

We're targeting Spring 2026 for the first closed cohort in E1, E14, EC, WC, and W1 postcodes. Exact date depends on finalising our regulatory position with UK authorities.

You'll get an email notification the moment onboarding opens in your postcode.

Email couriers@cashflow.app with the email you signed up with and the changes you need. We'll update your record and confirm.

Email privacy@cashflow.app from the email address you signed up with, subject line "Delete my data". We'll delete your record and confirm within 7 working days.

Alternatively, any waitlist email we send you includes an unsubscribe link that also triggers deletion.

About being a courier

Typical couriers in Zone 1 earn £25–40/hour during peak hours. Earnings depend on: your cash float, the fees you set, how busy your area is, and how long you stay online.

A typical Friday night in Zone 1: 6 deliveries × £14 average fee × 75% courier share = £63/hr. Add tips and surge pricing and peak earnings cross £35/hr regularly.

Every completed delivery pays into a digital wallet managed by our licensed payment partner. You can withdraw to your UK bank account any time, or let automatic weekly payouts run.

You'll receive a year-end earnings statement to simplify your tax return.

No. CashFlow couriers are self-employed independent contractors. You choose when to work and how much to work. You're responsible for registering with HMRC as self-employed and for your own income tax and National Insurance.

When onboarding opens, you'll need:

  • A valid government-issued photo ID (passport or UK driving licence)
  • Proof of right to work in the UK (BRP, settled status, or passport)
  • A UK bank account in your name
  • A smartphone (iOS 14+ or Android 9+)

Verification runs in 5 minutes via our identity partner. No paperwork, no in-person appointments.

Customers pay upfront through our licensed payment partner before a courier is dispatched, so you're never chasing payment. Every cash handover uses a two-factor QR and banknote verification. GPS tracking, in-app chat, and live support run throughout each delivery.

In the rare case of a dispute, we run a full review using transaction data, GPS trail, and chat history before deciding any outcome.

Using the app

CashFlow runs on iPhone (iOS 14 or later) and Android (9 or later). You can also use CashFlow in any modern browser on desktop or mobile.

Try these in order:

  1. Pull down to refresh the screen
  2. Check your internet connection
  3. Close the app completely and reopen it
  4. Uninstall and reinstall from the App Store

If none of these work, email hello@cashflow.app with your device model, OS version, and a screenshot. We'll respond within 1 working day.

Location — used to match couriers and customers nearby, show the right delivery area, and navigate. Only used when the app is in use; we do not track you in the background.

Camera — used for secure QR-code handover verification and to scan banknotes for authenticity checks. Not used otherwise.

Delete your account or data

You have the right to delete your CashFlow account and all associated data at any time. Under UK GDPR we'll process the request within 7 working days.

How to delete your data

Email privacy@cashflow.app from the email address on your account. Subject line: "Delete my account".

We'll delete your waitlist entry (or account, once CashFlow is operational) and confirm by reply. Where we must retain some information for legal reasons (e.g. accounting records for 6 years), we'll tell you what and why.
